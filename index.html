About Me

Hello! My name is Aiden Allen. I am an aspiring software engineer currently majoring in Computer Science at Florida State University. I have been programming since high school, and worked and posted numerous projects since. As I progressed through middle school, my interests shifted towards mathematics, and I envisioned a future as a mathematician. However, it was during this time that I stumbled upon the world of technology, and it captivated me instantly. I would stay awake late into the night, engrossed in videos that taught me how to assemble computers, and I eagerly filled my mom's Amazon cart with essential components like CPUs, GPUs, motherboards, and PSUs. Driven by this newfound love for technology, I made a significant decision during my sophomore year of high school: to enroll in a programming course. This course primarily focused on the fundamentals of Python. I dedicated countless hours to crafting intricate programs that exceeded the course requirements. My final project for this high school course can be found here.

As I entered college for Computer Science I continued to watch tutorial after tutorial learning everything I can about programming. My Freshman year I taught myself HTML, and started taking notes through my website. The site and source code of which are published on my github.

Spell Checker

This project was developed for my COP4530 course (Data structures 2). This project encompasses a hashtable library, and a spell checker, both of which were created for the purpose of this project. The spell checker is able to read in a dictionary, and compare it to a text file. The spell checker will then output the words that are not in the dictionary, and prompt the user to correct it (using FTXUI library). This project taught me valuble skills in data structures, and the importance of efficent code.
